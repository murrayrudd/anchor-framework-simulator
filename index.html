Institutional Anchor Framework: Time Preference Simulator

Rudd (2026) — Supply-side determinants of effective time preference under information velocity

About this model. Effective time preference ρ* is decomposed into an existence channel (φ_E) operating on three anchor stocks — biophysical (B), institutional (I), and historical (H) — and a salience channel (φ_Z) operating on the balance between information velocity (v) and deliberative capacity (D). Each anchor stock evolves with a depreciation rate that rises in v and a supply rate that rises in D; the biophysical stock has a state-dependent supply, generating hysteresis. Parameters not exposed below are held at framework defaults; see paper appendix for full specification.

Reference. Rudd, M.A. (2026). An institutional framework for modeling the supply side of time preference. SSRN preprint.
Source. All computations run client-side; no data is collected or transmitted. Code: single self-contained HTML file.
